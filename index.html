Introduction

Food distribution in mountainous regions is a complex issue due to the challenging terrain, isolation, and harsh weather conditions. One of the main problems is accessibility. Narrow, winding roads that are susceptible to landslides and avalanches make it difficult to transport food. Additionally, the limited infrastructure, including roads and storage facilities, hinders efficient distribution. Harsh weather conditions, like heavy snowfall and extreme temperatures, often disrupt food delivery efforts.
